## About the Research

Cryogenic Electron Tomography (cryo-ET) visualizes cell structures and macromolecules in their native environment. Accurate membrane segmentation is critical for understanding cellular organization, but low signal-to-noise ratio, missing wedge artifacts, and other noise sources make reliable segmentation challenging.

ETSAM addresses these challenges by fine-tuning SAM2's video-prediction architecture and using a cascaded two-stage design where each stage's output informs the next. The model was developed and tested by Joel Selvaraj and Jianlin Cheng at the University of Missouri.

:::note[Preprint]
Selvaraj & Cheng (2025). *ETSAM: Effectively Segmenting Cell Membranes in cryo-Electron Tomograms.* bioRxiv. [doi:10.1101/2025.11.23.689996](https://doi.org/10.1101/2025.11.23.689996)
:::
